# HC-TRUST-LAYER Trusted Relationship Model

## Purpose

This document defines trusted relationship foundations for HC-TRUST-LAYER and HC:// trust graph planning.

It is documentation-only guidance for machine-readable provenance and does not implement automatic trust decisions.

## Trusted Relationship Concepts

A trusted relationship is a reviewable linkage between entities where identity context, provenance continuity, and audit trail evidence are explicit.

Trusted relationship designation is advisory until human-supervised validation confirms interpretation.

## Validator-to-Validator Trust

Validator-to-validator trust relationships describe corroboration, disagreement visibility, and role-bounded interaction between validator entities.

These relationships should preserve scope and capability context without implying autonomous governance finality.

## Witness-to-Record Linkage

Witness-to-record linkage binds witness attestations to canonical record identifiers, observation scope, and relevant time context.

## Provenance-to-Package Linkage

Provenance-to-package linkage ties verification packages to lineage evidence, transformation context, and record continuity expectations.

## Federation Trust Linkage

Federation trust linkage documents relationships between origin and receiving federation contexts while preserving route provenance and audit trail continuity.

## Dispute-Aware Relationships

Dispute-aware relationships retain contested links and challenge metadata so review paths remain transparent and reversible.

## Revocation-Aware Relationships

Revocation-aware relationships preserve active, suspended, revoked, and superseded states in relationship metadata to prevent stale trust assumptions.

## Trust Continuity Concepts

Trust continuity concepts include:
- continuity markers across snapshots and exports
- explicit supersession and revocation linkage
- stable references for canonical record and provenance paths

## Graph Isolation Concepts

Graph isolation concepts include quarantining suspicious or unverified relationship clusters to reduce trust graph poisoning impact while preserving review evidence.

## Future Trust Anchor Concepts

Future trust anchor concepts may include explicit anchor relationship classes, anchor version lineage, and anchor transition traces.

No cryptographic implementation is introduced in this phase.
