# HC:// Governance Structure and Oversight Map

This document defines a structured governance and oversight map for HC:// in HC-TRUST-LAYER.

Scope boundaries:

- Documentation-only governance mapping.
- Advisory-only verification posture is preserved.
- Human-supervised validation remains required.
- No canonical schema changes.
- No validator or guard weakening.
- No centralized absolute authority claims.

## Purpose

Establish a clear, inspectable map of governance layers, participant roles, oversight relationships, and escalation paths so trust interpretation remains distributed, challengeable, and traceable.

## Governance layers

### Constitution Layer

Defines durable constitutional principles for HC://, including transparency, challengeability, provenance continuity, and anti-hidden-control boundaries.

### Foundation Layer

Defines baseline governance and verification posture in-repository, including guard continuity, trust-kernel boundaries, and documentation-first review controls.

### Operational Core Layer

Defines day-to-day operation boundaries for maintainers, validators, and reviewers, including review routing, evidence handling, and escalation hygiene.

### Federation Oversight Layer

Defines cross-organization and cross-context oversight where federation participants cross-review trust-impacting interpretations and preserve dispute visibility.

### Public Review Layer

Defines public-facing inspectability, public verifier participation, and challenge pathways for contested outcomes without granting unverifiable authority.

### Audit & Continuity Layer

Defines audit trail continuity expectations for governance actions, dispute transitions, reviewer accountability, and traceable governance evolution.

## Participant roles

- **maintainer:** stewards repository integrity, guardrail continuity, and trust-kernel boundaries while remaining auditable.
- **validator:** performs verification analysis and signals outcomes that remain challengeable and attributable.
- **federation validator:** performs independent federation cross-review and comparative interpretation across participants.
- **human reviewer:** performs human-supervised validation for consequential interpretation and dispute handling.
- **AI-assisted validator:** provides advisory analysis with visible uncertainty, explicit limitations, and traceable provenance context.
- **oversight participant:** participates in internal or external oversight, audit review, and escalation supervision.
- **public verifier:** inspects public verification evidence and may initiate challenge or dispute escalation.

## Oversight relationships

- Maintainers remain auditable through traceable review actions, attributable governance updates, and visible escalation handling.
- Validators remain challengeable through documented dispute pathways and inspectable rationale continuity.
- Federation participants cross-review each other to reduce concentrated interpretation risk and support distributed accountability.
- Disputes remain inspectable through visible chronology, preserved disagreement, and attributable resolution transitions.
- Governance changes remain traceable through documented rationale, versioned updates, and audit trail continuity.
- No single actor silently controls trust outcomes; consequential interpretation remains multi-layered and human-supervised.

## Escalation paths

### Public dispute escalation

Public verifier or reviewer dispute signals route to documented review intake, human-supervised triage, and visible dispute-state handling.

### Federation review escalation

Conflicting local and federation interpretations route to cross-participant escalation with preserved rationale, trace links, and attributable reconciliation steps.

### Emergency integrity escalation

Integrity-critical signals (for example suspected tampering or continuity rupture) route to high-priority reviewer escalation with explicit uncertainty markers and audit continuity preservation.

### Maintainer accountability escalation

Maintainer-impacting concerns route to attributable oversight review and documented accountability pathways without silent override privileges.

### Consensus conflict escalation

Material disagreement across AI-assisted validators, human reviewers, and federation validators routes to explicit conflict-state handling rather than forced consensus collapse.

## Governance safety concepts

- **distributed accountability:** trust interpretation responsibility is shared across roles and layers.
- **visible uncertainty:** uncertainty remains explicit rather than suppressed by authority language.
- **challengeable verification:** verification outcomes remain contestable and reviewable.
- **immutable-style governance history:** governance transitions preserve chronology and continuity awareness.
- **sponsor isolation:** sponsors cannot silently control validator or reviewer outcomes.
- **anti-hidden-control philosophy:** governance rejects opaque override channels and undisclosed authority concentration.

## Architectural progression

HC:// governance progression should remain staged and auditable:

1. **repository:** documentation-first governance clarity, guard continuity, and canonical boundary protection.
2. **protocol:** stable protocol semantics with explicit governance interpretation boundaries.
3. **validator ecosystem:** expanded validator and reviewer participation with attributable cross-review.
4. **distributed trust infrastructure:** broader federation operations with multi-layer oversight and preserved challengeability.

## Interpretation guidance

- Governance should remain inspectable through documented processes, attributable actions, and visible escalation states.
- Trust should emerge from traceability, provenance continuity, and auditable decision pathways rather than role assertions.
- Oversight should remain multi-layered so no single layer can silently dominate consequential interpretation.
- Visible disagreement is healthier than opaque authority because disagreement preserves challengeability and accountability.

## Implementation notes

- This map is documentation guidance and does not modify canonical records, schema contracts, validator logic, signing semantics, federation runtime behavior, policy evaluator behavior, or workflow security controls.
- This map preserves advisory-only verification and human-supervised validation for consequential trust interpretation.
