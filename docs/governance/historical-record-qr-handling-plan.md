# Historical Record QR Handling Plan

## Purpose

This document defines how historical record QR target artifacts should be preserved, deprecated, regenerated, or linked before any historical HC:// records move from `records/verified/` to `records/archived/`.

This is documentation-only governance guidance for archive migration planning. It does not move records, modify records, modify hash files, generate hash files, modify QR artifacts, generate QR artifacts, modify generated artifacts, modify release evidence records, change schemas, change validators, change workflows, change runtime behavior, or alter signing, federation, policy, or trust-kernel logic.

## Scope

This plan applies to the current historical record archive migration planning set:

- `records/verified/HC-TEST-2026-0001.md`
- `records/verified/HC-CHATGPT-2026-0001.md`
- `records/verified/HC-CHATGPT-2026-0002.md`

This plan was prepared after reviewing the archive readiness, blocker review, target-path decision, hash handling plan, and current `qr/` inventory. It complements the hash handling plan by focusing only on QR target artifacts and QR-related migration evidence.

## Path-sensitive QR risk

QR target artifacts are path-sensitive when they resolve to a concrete record URL or repository path. Moving a historical record from `records/verified/` to `records/archived/` can make a previously valid QR target stale, ambiguous, or dependent on platform redirects.

The current known path-sensitive QR risk is `qr/HC-CHATGPT-2026-0001.txt`, which points to the existing `records/verified/HC-CHATGPT-2026-0001.md` path. If the record later moves, maintainers must decide whether that QR target remains legacy evidence, is deprecated, is linked to a new target, or is superseded by newly generated QR material.

QR target changes can affect public verification expectations even when record content is unchanged. A future migration must therefore distinguish:

- legacy QR target evidence from new archived-path QR target evidence;
- access convenience from proof of truth;
- missing QR text artifacts from missing QR impact;
- regenerated QR material from preserved historical QR evidence; and
- documentation readiness from migration execution readiness.

## Missing QR artifact handling

Missing QR text artifacts are inventory findings only. They must not be treated as proof that no QR target, QR image, public link, browser bookmark, release note, downstream reference, or external scan path exists.

For this migration set:

- `qr/HC-CHATGPT-2026-0002.txt` is referenced by `records/verified/HC-CHATGPT-2026-0002.md` but is not present in `qr/`.
- `qr/HC-TEST-2026-0001.txt` is not present in `qr/`, while `records/verified/HC-TEST-2026-0001.md` references `/qr/` generally.

A future migration PR should record these as unresolved QR inventory findings unless maintainers explicitly decide otherwise. Agents must not fabricate QR evidence to fill the gap.

## Preservation options

Maintainers may choose one or more of the following options in a later migration-specific PR:

1. **Preserve legacy QR artifacts only.** Keep existing QR files unchanged and document that any path-bound targets refer to the pre-migration `records/verified/` location.
2. **Preserve legacy QR artifacts and add migration notes.** Keep existing QR files unchanged, then add reviewed documentation that records old target, new target, rationale, reviewer, and whether the legacy QR artifact is preserved or deprecated.
3. **Preserve legacy QR artifacts and add new archived-path QR targets.** Keep existing QR evidence unchanged, add new reviewed QR target artifacts for `records/archived/` paths, and link each new artifact back to the legacy target evidence.
4. **Deprecate legacy QR artifacts without deletion.** Keep old artifacts in place but document that they are legacy path-bound access evidence and are no longer preferred active targets after migration.
5. **Replace or overwrite legacy QR artifacts only with explicit maintainer approval.** This is the highest-risk option and should be avoided unless maintainers approve replacement in writing and preserve enough migration evidence to audit the old target, new target, command/tool used, reviewer, rationale, and disposition of old QR evidence.

No option should fabricate QR artifacts, scan results, reviewer approvals, migration timestamps, command output, signatures, or production-readiness claims.

## Recommended approach

The recommended approach is to treat existing QR files as legacy path-bound evidence and avoid silently overwriting them.

A future migration PR should:

- preserve `qr/HC-CHATGPT-2026-0001.txt` unless maintainers explicitly approve a different disposition;
- treat the current `records/verified/` target in `qr/HC-CHATGPT-2026-0001.txt` as legacy path-bound evidence;
- treat missing QR files for `HC-CHATGPT-2026-0002` and `HC-TEST-2026-0001` as inventory findings, not proof of no QR impact;
- inspect any binary QR-related artifacts before relying on them for migration decisions;
- if QR targets are changed later, document the old target, new target, command/tool used, reviewer, rationale, and whether old QR evidence is deprecated or preserved;
- link any new archived-path QR evidence back to the legacy target evidence instead of erasing earlier bindings; and
- avoid claims of autonomous governance finality, forensic certainty, truth finality, live federation guarantees, or production readiness.

## Non-goals

This plan does not:

- move historical records;
- modify historical records;
- modify existing hash files;
- generate new hash files;
- modify QR artifacts;
- generate QR artifacts;
- decode, validate, or certify binary QR images;
- modify generated artifacts;
- modify release evidence records;
- modify canonical records, schemas, validators, workflows, runtime code, signing logic, federation logic, policy files, or trust-kernel logic;
- decide hash handling, release evidence handling, or generated artifact regeneration by itself; or
- claim that migration is ready to execute.

## Pre-migration requirements

Before any historical record moves from `records/verified/` to `records/archived/`, maintainers should complete the following QR-specific requirements:

1. Confirm the migration PR scope and affected record IDs.
2. Confirm that `records/archived/` remains the approved target path.
3. Inventory all QR-related files under `qr/`, including text and binary artifacts.
4. Confirm whether each historical record has an explicit QR reference, a pending QR note, a general QR reference, or no QR reference.
5. Inspect existing QR target text artifacts and record current targets before changing any target.
6. Decide whether legacy QR artifacts will be preserved only, deprecated in documentation, linked to new evidence, or accompanied by new archived-path QR targets.
7. Define the exact command or tool that will be used if any QR target artifact is generated or regenerated.
8. Define where QR migration evidence will live and how it links to legacy target evidence.
9. Identify the reviewer responsible for validating QR handling decisions.
10. Record unresolved missing QR artifact findings before merge.
11. Run terminology, docs drift, canonical artifact, validator, and migration-specific checks required by the migration PR.
12. Record human-supervised validation before treating QR handling as accepted.

## Future migration checklist

A future migration PR should include a QR handling checklist similar to this:

- [ ] Existing QR artifacts are preserved or any replacement has explicit maintainer approval.
- [ ] Each moved record lists its old path and new path.
- [ ] Each path-bound QR target lists its old target and intended new target, if any.
- [ ] Missing QR text artifacts are recorded as inventory findings.
- [ ] Binary QR artifacts are inspected before being relied on for migration decisions.
- [ ] Any QR generation or regeneration command/tool is recorded exactly as used.
- [ ] Any new QR evidence is reviewable and not fabricated.
- [ ] New QR evidence links back to legacy target evidence where applicable.
- [ ] Hash handling is reviewed separately.
- [ ] Release evidence `related_records` impact is reviewed separately.
- [ ] Generated artifact regeneration is performed by approved tooling, not hand editing.
- [ ] Human-supervised validation notes are included before migration is treated as accepted.

## Human-supervised validation notes

QR handling for archive migration must remain human-supervised. Agents and automation may inventory files, report path-sensitive targets, suggest commands, and prepare reviewable documentation, but they must not fabricate QR evidence, fabricate approvals, overwrite legacy artifacts silently, or treat a migration as accepted without maintainer review.

Validation should explicitly distinguish:

- preserved legacy QR evidence from newly generated archived-path QR evidence;
- QR access paths from record truth or forensic certainty;
- unresolved inventory gaps from confirmed absence of QR impact;
- advisory agent output from maintainer approval; and
- documentation readiness from migration execution readiness.

Until a future maintainer-approved migration PR completes these steps, the historical records and their existing QR artifacts should remain unchanged.
