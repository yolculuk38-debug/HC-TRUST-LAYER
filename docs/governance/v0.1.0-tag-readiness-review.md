## Known limitations accepted

Known limitations accepted for tag preparation:

- v0.1.0 is early-stage advisory HC:// verification infrastructure, not production-ready infrastructure.
- HC:// verification reflects repository-defined integrity and provenance signals, not objective truth.
- Human-supervised validation remains part of the governance boundary.
- Witness and trust scoring remain experimental.
- Runtime/API surfaces remain advisory and must not be represented as production-ready.
- Local runtime/API test execution remains environment-limited in the recorded CI/test evidence; clean GitHub Actions or equivalent reviewer-visible CI evidence should be confirmed before publication.
- `docs/verify.html` remains a first-flow/demo static QR verification page only and does not verify arbitrary records.
- v0.1.0 does not provide a hosted general public verifier for arbitrary records.
- Non-demo QR routes remain advisory/navigation placeholders unless separately deployed and validated.
- Legacy hash manifests, legacy QR artifacts, release evidence path bindings, and generated audit snapshot evidence remain preserved with documented historical or pending-context limitations.
- v0.1.0 does not claim security certification, forensic certainty, truth finality, autonomous governance, live federation guarantees, complete dispute automation, final trust scoring semantics, final validator semantics, or cryptographic or policy guarantees beyond repository evidence.

These limitations are acceptable for tag preparation because they are consistently disclosed in the reviewed release-governance materials and do not require protected-path or evidence-bearing artifact mutation before tag preparation.
