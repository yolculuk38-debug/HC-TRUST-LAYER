# HC:// Validator Ethics and Conduct Framework

This document defines ethical, operational, and accountability expectations for HC:// validators, reviewers, maintainers, AI-assisted analysis systems, and federation participants in HC-TRUST-LAYER.

Scope boundaries:

- Documentation-only governance framework.
- Advisory-only verification posture is preserved.
- Human-supervised validation remains required.
- No canonical schema changes.
- No validator or guard weakening.
- No claims of perfect neutrality, infallibility, or objective certainty.

## Purpose

Trust systems require accountable participants whose actions remain inspectable, traceable, and challengeable. This framework establishes shared conduct expectations so governance trust in HC:// remains earned through transparent practice rather than assumed through role labels.

## Participant categories

- **human reviewer**
- **AI-assisted validator**
- **federation validator**
- **maintainer**
- **oversight participant**
- **public reviewer**

All categories remain subject to audit visibility, challenge pathways, and human-supervised validation boundaries.

## Expected conduct

Participants are expected to preserve the following conduct baseline:

- **transparency:** material review and validator actions should remain visible and attributable.
- **traceable actions:** trust-impacting actions should preserve an auditable trail.
- **conflict disclosure:** conflicts, incentives, and role overlaps should be disclosed.
- **evidence preservation:** verification-relevant evidence should be preserved and continuity-aware.
- **visible uncertainty:** uncertainty should be explicit instead of hidden by certainty language.
- **challenge acceptance:** challenged outcomes should remain reviewable and contestable.
- **audit cooperation:** participants should cooperate with legitimate audit and oversight processes.
- **escalation responsibility:** unresolved risk, conflict, or ambiguity should be escalated through documented pathways.

## Prohibited behavior

The HC:// ethics and conduct baseline prohibits the following:

- **hidden override**
- **undisclosed sponsor influence**
- **fake verification claims**
- **forged audit activity**
- **silent evidence removal**
- **deceptive certainty**
- **fabricated AI-authority claims**
- **reviewer impersonation**
- **tampering concealment**

Any detected prohibited behavior should trigger visible escalation and attributable review handling.

## Reviewer integrity concepts

Reviewer integrity in HC-TRUST-LAYER requires:

- **reviewer accountability:** consequential reviewer decisions remain attributable.
- **review traceability:** reviewer rationale and review transitions remain auditable.
- **confidence disclosure:** confidence level and limitations are explicitly declared.
- **disagreement visibility:** disagreement remains visible rather than silently flattened.
- **escalation pathways:** unresolved disagreement routes through documented escalation.
- **audit visibility:** reviewer-impacting events remain inspectable.
- **conflict transparency:** conflicts and incentive risks are disclosed.

## AI validator conduct concepts

AI-assisted validator conduct in HC:// requires:

- **advisory-only posture:** AI output cannot independently finalize consequential trust outcomes.
- **explainable outputs:** AI-assisted conclusions should include inspectable rationale and boundaries.
- **traceable provenance:** AI outputs should retain attributable provenance continuity.
- **model disclosure:** validator model/process context should be disclosed where governance-relevant.
- **visible uncertainty:** uncertainty, ambiguity, and confidence limits remain explicit.
- **no unverifiable authority claims:** AI systems must not claim autonomous authority without repository-backed validation.

## Federation ethics concepts

Federation ethics in HC:// requires:

- **independent oversight:** federation participants should preserve independent review capacity.
- **cross-validator review:** trust-impacting claims should remain open to cross-validator scrutiny.
- **anti-capture philosophy:** governance should resist concentrated control by single actors.
- **distributed accountability:** responsibility should be shared across participants and layers.
- **challengeable governance:** federation interpretation outcomes should remain contestable and auditable.

## Governance interpretation guidance

- Trust systems require accountable participants; role labels alone do not establish trust.
- No validator should operate above inspection, challenge, or audit visibility.
- Visible disagreement is healthier than hidden manipulation because it preserves challengeability.
- Governance trust must remain earned through inspectable conduct, not assumed through institutional posture.

## Implementation notes

- This framework is documentation guidance and does not modify canonical records, schema contracts, validator logic, signing semantics, federation runtime behavior, policy evaluator behavior, or workflow security controls.
- This framework preserves advisory-only verification and human-supervised validation for consequential interpretation.
- This framework reinforces transparency, inspectability, provenance continuity, and audit trail expectations.
