## Purpose

This document clarifies what HC:// Foundation Layer capabilities are completed in-repository, what remains partially implemented, and how HC-TRUST-LAYER transitions into the next architecture phases.

This status is evidence-bound to repository documentation and guardrails. It does not introduce production-readiness claims, truth guarantees, or autonomous governance finality.

Repository evidence supports Foundation Layer baseline completion for:

These areas should continue through bounded, reversible, human-supervised increments with explicit trust-impact review.

## Next-Phase Transition

With Foundation Layer baseline completed, HC-TRUST-LAYER transitions to phased expansion across:

1. **Constitution Layer**
   - Stabilize constitutional interpretation boundaries.
   - Preserve advisory-only verification and review-layer accountability.
2. **Operational Core Layer**
   - Harden operator workflows, diagnostics, and reproducibility tooling.
   - Keep guardrails explicit, auditable, and non-bypassable.
3. **AI Trust Security Layer**
   - Expand explainable AI-assisted analysis within constrained trust boundaries.
   - Preserve human-supervised validation for non-trivial trust outcomes.
4. **Distributed Trust Network**
   - Progress federation and validator interoperability in documented phases.
   - Maintain provenance continuity and anti-spoofing alignment.
5. **Public Trust Ecosystem**
   - Improve public verification UX and review transparency.
   - Expand accountability surfaces before autonomy claims.

## Architectural Progression

HC:// progression should remain staged and auditable:

- **repository:** documentation-first governance, guardrail continuity, and canonical boundary protection.
- **protocol:** stable verification semantics before broad automation.
- **validator ecosystem:** gradual reviewer/validator expansion with visible accountability.
- **distributed trust infrastructure:** phased federation and networked trust operations after foundation maturity.

## Roadmap Philosophy

The HC-TRUST-LAYER transition follows these ordering principles:

- foundation before scale
- governance before mass adoption
- accountability before authority
- transparency before automation
- trust through traceability
