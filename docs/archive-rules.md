# Archive Rules

## Humanity Chain — Open Verification Protocol

Version: 0.1  
Status: Experimental

---

## Purpose

Humanity Chain archives public human-AI interaction records using transparent documentation, chronological commits, and hash-based reference structures.

The purpose of the archive is documentation and verification — not institutional endorsement.

---

## Core Principles

- Data before narrative
- Public verification over hidden claims
- Chronological documentation
- Transparent archive structure
- Equal visibility of records

---

## Archive Rule

Rule: No commit, no record.

Only committed and publicly archived records are considered valid within the Humanity Chain archive structure.

---

## AI Interaction Policy

AI-generated responses archived in Humanity Chain do not represent:

- autonomous participation
- institutional partnership
- legal agreement
- official endorsement
- contractual involvement

All AI responses are archived and documented by human participants.

---

## Moderation Principles

The following content may be removed or restricted:

- hate speech
- racism
- religious attacks
- targeted harassment
- spam and flooding
- fake verification records
- impersonation attempts
- violent extremist propaganda

Freedom of record does not mean freedom from responsibility.

---

## Verification Logic

Hashes represent archived response snapshots at the time of recording.

Hashes are used for reference and verification purposes only.

---

## Transparency Notice

Humanity Chain is an experimental open-source archive protocol.

The system may evolve over time through public documentation and transparent revision history.
