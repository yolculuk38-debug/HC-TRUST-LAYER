# HC-TRUST-LAYER Evidence Continuity (Foundation)

## Status

- documentation-only architecture foundation
- no production continuity automation in this phase
- no truth guarantee language

## Continuity Overview

Evidence continuity in HC-TRUST-LAYER preserves interpretability of verification artifacts across time, systems, and policy evolution.

The focus is durable provenance, traceable audit trail linkage, and stable canonical record references for human-supervised validation.

## Historical Verification Continuity

Historical verification continuity keeps past outcomes reviewable under their original context.

Concepts:

- preserve historical policy and validator context
- retain historical verification snapshot references
- keep dispute and revocation states visible by time period
- avoid rewriting historical evidence outcomes

## Archive Migration Continuity

Archive migration continuity preserves evidence meaning during storage or format transitions.

Concepts:

- pre/post migration integrity checks
- deterministic mapping logs for migrated fields
- provenance of custody transfer events
- rollback references for critical migration incidents

## Provenance Continuity

Provenance continuity preserves lineage from source context to current state.

Concepts:

- stable provenance identifiers
- explicit parent/child and derivative references
- degradation markers for missing lineage segments
- federation-aware provenance reconciliation notes

## Snapshot Continuity

Snapshot continuity maintains reproducibility across verification points in time.

Concepts:

- snapshot identifier persistence
- policy and capability context at snapshot time
- compatibility notes for snapshot schema evolution
- links from snapshots to canonical record and dispute state

## Replay Continuity Risks

Replay continuity risks arise when artifacts are reintroduced without clear lineage.

Risk classes:

- duplicate evidence ingestion without linkage
- replay timing ambiguity across federated exchanges
- replayed artifacts presented without revocation context
- lineage splitting that obscures adverse history

## Revocation Continuity

Revocation continuity ensures revoked or superseded evidence remains visible historically.

Concepts:

- non-destructive revocation markers
- revocation-to-origin linkage
- revocation propagation notes across federation boundaries
- retained references in audit trail views

## Long-Term Evidence Integrity

Long-term evidence integrity preserves cryptographic and contextual interpretability.

Concepts:

- deterministic hash continuity planning
- algorithm migration transparency
- archival redundancy and integrity checking cadence
- incident logs for corruption, recovery, or uncertainty events

## Chain-of-Reference Concepts

Chain-of-reference links evidence artifacts into inspectable continuity paths.

Concepts:

- canonical record anchor references
- provenance, witness, validator, dispute, and snapshot links
- signed reference manifests where supported
- continuity health indicators for incomplete chains
